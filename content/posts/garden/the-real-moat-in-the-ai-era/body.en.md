My current thesis for the AI era is simple: the real value will not live in the model alone. It will live in **team-level context**, in **agentified workflows**, and in a growing layer of **decentralized custom tools**.

**1. Team-level context**
Personal productivity is the shallow end. The deeper moat is shared context: how a team makes decisions, where truth lives, which tradeoffs are acceptable, what "done" means, who approves what, and how work moves across functions. Models are becoming cheaper and more interchangeable. Context is not. A team that can structure, expose, and maintain its context will outperform a team with better prompts but fragmented memory.

**2. Agentify the workflow**
The next step is not to chat more with AI. It is to wrap recurring work in agents: triage, drafting, QA, research, routing, follow-up, handoff. Good agents are not magic personalities; they are durable interfaces over context, tools, and policies. Once a workflow is agentified, the unit of leverage shifts from one person’s output to the system’s throughput.

**3. Decentralized custom tools**
I do not think one central AI product will fit every team. The winning pattern is a loose ecosystem of small, composable tools built close to the work: a sales copilot for one pipeline, a release agent for engineering, a spec checker for PMs, an internal memory layer for support. Local tools capture local reality. Central platforms should provide rails, not dictate the last mile.

If I were building in this moment, I would start by mapping hidden team context, then pick one painful recurring workflow, then build the narrowest tool that makes the team faster <u>together</u>. In AI, scale will come from context-rich systems, not generic chat windows.

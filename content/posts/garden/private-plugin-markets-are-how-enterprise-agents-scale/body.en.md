The February 24, 2026 Cowork plugins note made me think about enterprise agents from a less glamorous angle: real adoption is usually a **distribution problem**, not a model problem.

Inside a company, the hard part is rarely "can the model call tools?" The hard part is:

- who can discover the right tool,
- who can trust it,
- who maintains it,
- how permissions are scoped,
- and how local workflow knowledge stops dying inside one team.

That is why private plugin marketplaces matter.

They turn scattered internal tooling into something closer to a usable operating layer:

- tools become **discoverable** instead of tribal,
- integrations become **reusable** instead of one-off,
- permissions become **governable** instead of implicit,
- and good workflows become **distributable** across the company.

In that sense, the marketplace is not a side feature. It is where agent capability becomes organizational memory.

I suspect many enterprise agent products will learn the same lesson: the winning surface is not the smartest chat window. It is the cleanest path from **intent -> approved tool -> repeatable workflow -> measured outcome**.

That is a much better moat than a demo.
